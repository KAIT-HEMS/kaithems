title: "A mutual cooperation and collaboration agreement with the College of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science and the Institute of Energy Engineering at National Central University"
date: 2024-10-28
summary: "A mutual cooperation and collaboration agreement was concluded with the College of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science and the Institute of Energy Engineering at National Central University."
---

On October 21, 2024, the Smart House Research Center of Kanagawa Institute of Technology concluded mutual cooperation and collaboration agreements with two departments at National Central University (Taoyuan City, Taiwan)—the College of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science and the Center for Energy Research—to promote and support a joint research project on energy management systems.

Our center was established in 2012 and has been promoting research and dissemination activities related to “ECHONET Lite (ISO/IEC 14543-4-3),” an international standard communication protocol for IoT.

“ECHONET Lite (ISO/IEC 14543-4-3)” is a standard implementation in ZEH (Net Zero Energy Houses (*1)), one of Japan’s smart housing models. The promotion of ZEH is one of the key policies for achieving carbon neutrality in Japan’s residential sector. The Smart House Research Center collaborates with the ECHONET Consortium, the organization responsible for formulating the ECHONET Lite specifications, in efforts to promote its adoption and advance international standardization (*2).

In recent years, Taiwan declared its commitment to achieving carbon neutrality in April 2021 and has been placing significant emphasis on research and development in the energy sector.

As part of future activities, students from laboratories in the Department of Electrical Engineering at National Central University will participate in short-term study programs at Kanagawa Institute of Technology. Through these programs, they will gain exposure to Japan’s cutting-edge initiatives in smart houses, energy management systems, and ECHONET Lite communication technologies, and begin exploring opportunities for future joint research between the two universities.
